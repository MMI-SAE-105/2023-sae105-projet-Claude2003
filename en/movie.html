"Black Panther" - The Odyssey Breaking Superhero Boundaries

By Claude MALANDA

Published on 24/10/2024, updated today at 9:45 AM

Released in 2018, Black Panther has become more than just a superhero film but rather a cultural reference. Directed by Ryan Coogler, it quickly gained impactful cultural significance with the spotlight on T'Challa, the king of Wakanda.

The film focuses on the fictional kingdom of Wakanda, a secretive and technologically advanced African nation. Wakanda is rich in vibranium, an extremely precious metal with exceptional properties. The story revolves around the Wakandan royal family, especially T'Challa, portrayed by Chadwick Boseman, who is the crown prince and later the king of Wakanda.

The story begins after the events of "Captain America: Civil War" when T'Challa returns to Wakanda to take his place on the throne after his father's death. He also becomes the superhero Black Panther by ingesting the heart-shaped herb, which grants superhuman abilities.

However, Wakanda's peace is threatened by external forces, including the villain Erik Killmonger, played by Michael B. Jordan, who has a familial connection to the royal family and seeks to overthrow T'Challa.

The power and strength of women are extraordinarily highlighted in the film, and this has had a significant impact on the audience. The film demonstrates that leadership has no gender, showcasing women such as Nakia, a strong-willed woman with a solid conscience; Shuri, an incredibly intelligent scientist who is Black Panther's sister and, at just 16 years old, is in charge of Wakanda's advanced technology; and warriors like Okoye, the leader of the formidable Dora Milaje, an intrepid force in protecting Wakanda.

These women are not mere side characters but hold central and influential roles in the plot. The film takes the initiative to expand the role of women in the superhero world by presenting them as powerful leaders, innovators, and protectors of their nation. This positive representation of women is a key element of what makes "Black Panther" a revolutionary film in the superhero genre.

The film has a significant impact in the scientific domain through how it portrays characters like Shuri, as a brilliant scientist and innovator. Shuri is the technological brain behind Wakanda, creating impressive scientific advancements. Her role in the film has inspired discussions about promoting women in sciences, especially minorities.

The impact of this film in the scientific field lies in its potential to generate interest among youth in the sciences, promote diversity in these fields, and stimulate reflection on future technological advancements.

Breaking stereotypes! That's what the film Black Panther proposes, offering a better representation of African Americans, an imaginary Africa, technologically prosperous, and an inspiring leader who embodies dignity and wisdom.
