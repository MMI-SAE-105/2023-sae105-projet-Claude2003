Bibliography of Ta-Nehisi Coates

Ta-Nehisi Coates is a renowned African-American author and essayist, whose powerful writing explores themes such as race, society, and the condition of African-Americans. The timeline below presents some of his most impactful works.

2021

The Water Dancer

I want what I've always wanted, what I've always asserted that I want. I want my hands, my legs, my arms, my smile, my entire body to belong to me and me alone.

2019

The Savage Sword of Conan, Volume 1

I was working hard. I was a man esteemed as a slave - in other words, I was not esteemed as a man.

2018

Eight Years in Power

All African Americans, regardless of their level of education and economic status, are in danger in America simply because of their skin color.

2017

The Trial of America

Celebrating freedom and democracy while forgetting that America originates from the economy of slavery is selective patriotism.

2017

The Big Fight

Everyone needs myths. And here, in the Far West of Baltimore where we had lost faith, where barbaric law prevailed.

2015

Between the World and Me

Never forget that we have been slaves in this country longer than we have been free.
